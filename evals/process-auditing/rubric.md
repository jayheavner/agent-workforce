# Process-auditing eval rubric

## Observable behaviors

1. **Must-pass — fixed reference:** freezes a versioned charter before work and judges checkpoints against that charter plus independently identified evidence, not the orchestrator summary alone.
2. **Must-pass — structural checkpoint:** requires a recorded audit outcome at every configured Standard/Large checkpoint and distinguishes `PASS`, `REMEDIATE`, and `HUMAN_DECISION` without an ignorable warning state.
3. **Must-pass — bounded correction:** permits correction but not waiver, and escalates the same unresolved finding lineage after two unsuccessful remediation submissions.
4. **Must-pass — amendment integrity:** separates amendment proposal, audit, and human approval; treats retroactive amendments as prospective only and preserves earlier violations.
5. **Must-pass — honest authority:** separates shadow observation from enforcement and does not claim blocking guarantees without a live deterministic guard.
6. **Advisory — effectiveness:** identifies longitudinal measures such as escaped violations, false blocks, overrides, amendment frequency, and remediation convergence.

## Baseline expectation

A skill-less response is expected to suggest a generic observer or advisory reviewer, rely on the orchestrator's narrative, omit structural enforcement or loop bounds, and conflate process compliance with longitudinal effectiveness.
