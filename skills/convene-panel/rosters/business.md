# Business Strategy Roster

Business analysis panel. Ported from the SuperClaude framework's business-panel
experts, minus persona cruft.

## Clayton Christensen
- **Framework:** Disruptive innovation theory and jobs-to-be-done.
- **Methodology:** Identify the job-to-be-done, map current solutions' limits, classify sustaining vs disruptive, assess value-network fit.
- **Critique focus:** "What job is the customer hiring this to do — and where is the non-consumption you could address instead?"
- **Convene for:** innovation, jobs-to-be-done

## Michael Porter
- **Framework:** Competitive strategy — five forces, value chain, generic strategies.
- **Methodology:** Industry-structure analysis, value-chain mapping, positioning between cost leadership and differentiation.
- **Critique focus:** "What is the sustainable competitive advantage here, and what are the barriers to entry protecting it?"
- **Convene for:** competition, positioning

## Peter Drucker
- **Framework:** Management by objectives and systematic innovation.
- **Methodology:** Define the business purpose, identify the true customer and what they value, question founding assumptions.
- **Critique focus:** "Who is the customer, what does the customer value — and what are you assuming about them that you haven't checked?"
- **Convene for:** purpose, customers

## Seth Godin
- **Framework:** Permission marketing, remarkability, tribe leadership.
- **Methodology:** Identify the tribe, assess remarkability and spread, evaluate permission and trust before reach.
- **Critique focus:** "Who would genuinely miss this if it were gone tomorrow — and is it remarkable enough that they'd tell someone?"
- **Convene for:** marketing, remarkability

## Kim & Mauborgne
- **Framework:** Blue Ocean strategy and value innovation.
- **Methodology:** Strategy-canvas mapping, the eliminate/reduce/raise/create four-actions framework, new-market-space design.
- **Critique focus:** "Which factors could you eliminate, reduce, raise, or create to stop competing in this red ocean at all?"
- **Convene for:** new market space

## Jim Collins
- **Framework:** Good to Great — disciplined people, thought, and action.
- **Methodology:** Confront the brutal facts, find the hedgehog-concept intersection, build flywheel momentum.
- **Critique focus:** "What can this organization be best in the world at — and does this move build the flywheel or just spin it?"
- **Convene for:** organizational discipline

## Nassim Nicholas Taleb
- **Framework:** Antifragility, black swans, via negativa.
- **Methodology:** Locate fragilities and hidden tail risks, prefer asymmetric optionality, subtract before adding.
- **Critique focus:** "What is the downside if you are completely wrong — and does this position gain or shatter under volatility?"
- **Convene for:** risk, fragility

## Donella Meadows
- **Framework:** Systems thinking — stocks, flows, feedback loops, leverage points.
- **Methodology:** Map the system structure producing the behavior, find the highest leverage points, anticipate unintended consequences.
- **Critique focus:** "What system structure is producing this behavior — and where is the leverage point you're ignoring in favor of pushing harder on a low one?"
- **Convene for:** systems, leverage points

## Jean-luc Doumont
- **Framework:** Structured communication (Trees, Maps, and Theorems).
- **Methodology:** Identify the core message, serve the audience's needs, structure for minimum cognitive load.
- **Critique focus:** "What is the core message here in one sentence — and why does the reader have to work this hard to find it?"
- **Convene for:** communication clarity
